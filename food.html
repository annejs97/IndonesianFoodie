Cucur Cake

×

Ingredients

  • 500 ml coconut milk
  • 1 pandan lead
  • 50 gr Hunkwe flour
  • 25 gr rice flour
  • 1/4 teaspon vanilla
  • 1/4 teaspon salt
  • 2 tablespoon white sugar
  • pandan paste
  • Red food coloring

Instruction

  1. Cook fresh water until boiling, put sliced brown sugar and white sugar, stirring until all brown sugar and white sugar dissolves. Turn off heat, removed, and filtered to a solution of sugar water clean of debris. Set aside.
  2. In a medium bowl the wheat flour and rice flour mixed and stirred till become a homogeneous mixture, pour the sugar solution slowly, stirring until the sugar solution all gone, stirring constantly, until batter (for the cake) is smooth and well blended. Then the mixture being patted for about 5 minutes so the batter is completely unified. After that the mixture let stand about 20 minutes.
  3. Preheat 50 ml oil in a frying pan with a small concave.
  4. Stirred the batter (for the cake) then take a vegetables spoon into the hot oil. During frying, the center of the batter flush of hot oil in order to mature evenly. Cake does not need to be reversed. Once cooked, removed and drained.
  5. Perform the four step so that all the fried dough out. For the material of the above we can make the cake as much as 30 cucur cake.
  6. It is easy, is not it ? It is recommended to serve while warm with a bitter tea.
  7. For variation, you can add, into the batter for the cake, smooth shred of durian fruit, or small pieces of jackfruit, which can lead to cucur cake with a distinctive taste.

Cenil

×

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 175 grams tapioca flour
  • Food coloring to taste

Ingredients For Biang

  • 150 ml water
  • 50 grams tapioca flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Ingredients For Sprinkling

  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 100 grams of medium coconut
  • 1 piece of solid leaves
  • 1 tablespoon granulated sugar

Instruction

  1. If the ingredients are all ready, now you can directly make cenil. First, please prepare a pot that will be used for cooking the culprit. Then put the culprit material into it. Stir until evenly distributed.
  2. Next, please boil the ingredients while stirring until thick and boiling like glue. If it is like that, turn off the heat and leave it for a while to warm.
  3. Then the tapioca flour is inserted little by little while continuing to knead until the dough can be collected.
  4. After that, the dough please divide it into 4 parts. For each section add a different food coloring. Then knead each dough again until it is evenly mixed.
  5. Please shape the dough as big as a little finger with both ends in a pursed shape or other shape according to taste.
  6. Next step please bring the water to a boil in a saucepan. After the water boils, please put the cenil that you have formed, boil until cooked and float. If it is like that, remove and drain first.
  7. After the cenil is cooked, the next step is to make the sprinkling material. Please mix the shredded coconut with salt. Then steam together with pandan leaves for approximately 10 minutes. If you have, remove and let stand for a while until warm.
  8. Then please boiled cenil toppled on the steamed grated coconut.
  9. Then sprinkle using granulated sugar. And ready to serve.

Bika Ambon

×

Ingredients

  • 330 ml coconut milk, thick
  • 100 ml water, lukewarm
  • 50 gr plain flour
  • 2 teaspoons dry yeast
  • 150 gr rice flour
  • 100 fr tapioca flour
  • 250 gr caster sugar
  • 180 ml coconut water
  • 5 egg yolks
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla essence
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instruction

  1. Put thick coconut milk in a small saucepan and bring to a low boil over very low heat. Remove and leave aside to cool completely.
  2. Combine lukewarm water, flour and instant yeast in a small bowl and leave aside to froth.
  3. Mix both flours, sugar, coconut water, egg yolks, essence and salt in a mixing bowl.
  4. Add fermented yeast mixture and the cooled coconut milk to mix.
  5. Leave mixture aside, covered with a damp tea towel to ferment for 2-3 hours.
  6. Turn batter into a lightly greased tray and bake in preheated oven at 200°C and bake until the top of the cake is golden brown.

Lapis kojo

×

Ingredients

Coconut milk dough:

  • 1 Liter thick coconut milk
  • 500 g granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 100 ml pandan suji water
  • 200 g margarine, melted

Egg Dough:

  • 20 eggs
  • 300 ml sweet thick creamer
  • 120 g plain flour
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Instruction

  1. Coconut milk dough: Mix well all the ingredients of the coconut milk dough in a saucepan, cook until boiling. Remove, leaving until the hot steam is gone.
  2. Egg batter: Beat the eggs with whisk until foamy, add coconut milk dough, beat well. Add the sweetened thick creamer, beat well.
  3. Add the flour and vanilla extract, beat well. Prepare a square baking sheet of 20x20x7 cm, grease with a little oil and sprinkle a little flour. Pour 1 spoonful of vegetable dough, flatten.
  4. Bake in the oven over a heat of 180⁰C until the surface is browned for ±10 minutes. Pour the dough over it, bake again until browned. Do the same until the dough runs out.
  5. Bake on low heat for 25 minutes until cooked through. Remove from the oven, cool.
  6. Remove the cake from the baking sheet, cut into pieces to taste. Serve.

Clorot

×

Ingredients

  • 175 gr brown sugar
  • 400 ml coconut milk
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 125 gr rice flour
  • 50 gr tapioca flour
  • Clorot cake mold from banana leaves/coconut leaves

Instruction

  1. The shape of a banana or coconut leaf forms a cone. Set aside.
  2. In a saucepan, add the brown sugar, salt and coconut milk. Stir until smooth then cook until brown sugar dissolves and boils.
  3. Then strain the dough and refrigerate.
  4. Prepare a large container, then add tapioca flour, rice flour and the cooled brown sugar solution. Stir until there are no lumps.
  5. Heat the steamer until the steamed water boils, then put the banana or coconut leaf mold that has been shaped.
  6. Pour the mixture into the mold until it is full.
  7. Steam for 20 minutes until cooked. and serve clorot

  • 7 quail eggs
  • 1/4 tsp salt

  • enough margarine for greasing

Instruction

Gudeg

×

Ingredients

  • 500 grams of young jackfruit, cut into pieces
  • 1 teabag
  • 6 guava leaves
  • 1 segment of galangal, crushed
  • 1 lemongrass stalk
  • 5 bay leaves
  • 3 lime leaves
  • 2 blocks of brown sugar
  • 1 pack of Kara brand instant coconut milk
  • 1500 ml of water
  • Salt and broth powder, to taste
  • 10 cloves of red onion
  • 5 cloves of garlic
  • 3 hazelnuts
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 segment of sand ginger

Instruction

  1. Prepare materials. Boil young jackfruit and give a little vegetable oil to get the sap out. Cook over high heat for about 10 minutes.
  2. Then, drain and discard the boiled water. Prepare a container, add young jackfruit, water, and tea bags. Cook until boiling.
  3. Saute ground spices, galangal, lemon grass, bay leaf, and lime leaves until cooked and fragrant. Then, pour it into the jackfruit stew and don't forget to throw away the teabags first
  4. Add brown sugar and guava leaves. Cook for up to 1 hour, stirring occasionally. Wait until the water remains a little and feels soft. Make sure it's not too dry, but that there's still some water left.
  5. Enter the instant coconut milk, then stir until evenly distributed. Cook for another 2 hours.
  6. Gudeg typical of Yogyakarta is ready to be served along with warm white rice, chicken, chili sauce, and krecek.

Pempek

×

Ingredients

  • 10 tbsp wheat flour
  • 3 tbsp rice flour
  • 8 tbsp sago flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp mushroom stock
  • 6 chicken eggs
  • enough sago flour to knead
  • enough hot water

Instruction

  1. Mix flour, rice flour, sago flour, salt, and mushroom broth. Stir well.
  2. Pour the hot water that has just been boiling into the dough little by little while kneading until all the ingredients come together. Let the dough cool.
  3. Add sago flour to the dough to knead until it is easy to shape.
  4. Divide the dough into 6 parts. Form a flat round, and make a pocket to put the eggs.
  5. Bring the water to a boil.
  6. Give the egg filling to the pempek dough. Tighten and trim the edges.
  7. Boil the pempek for about 20 minutes on medium heat. Lift and drain.
  8. After pempek cool, fry until brown.
  9. Serve pempek with cuko sauce and cucumber according to taste.

Sate Lilit

×

Ingredients

  • 500 gr chicken
  • 3 lemongrass stalks
  • 3 large red chilies
  • 10 red chilies
  • 1 sheet of lime leaf
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Shrimp paste
  • Powdered broth
  • Salt
  • Grated coconut
  • Palm sugar
  • 12 cloves of red onion
  • 8 cloves of garlic
  • 1 segment sand ginger
  • 1 segment of galangal
  • 1 segment of roasted turmeric
  • Ginger
  • 3 pecans
  • Pepper to taste
  • Coconut oil

Instruction

  1. Finely chop all the genep spices using a food processor into one
  2. Meanwhile, thinly slice the lemongrass, large red chilies and cayenne pepper
  3. Saute the genep seasoning and thin slices of lemongrass and chilies together.
  4. Add lime leaves, bay leaf, and shrimp paste
  5. Also add the stock powder and salt. Stir well
  6. Meanwhile, puree the chicken
  7. Mix the mashed chicken with three tablespoons of the genep seasoning that has been stir-fried earlier
  8. Add grated coconut, palm sugar. Stir into one
  9. Add more basic genep seasoning if you think it's still lacking.
  10. After the meat and spices are well mixed, add the fried chili sauce (optional).
  11. Then, wrap the satay meat on a skewer or lemongrass stalk
  12. Grill over coals, make sure all sides are perfectly cooked
  13. The satay wrap is ready to be served.

Rendang

×

Ingredients

  • 500 grams of meat
  • 1 stalk lemongrass geprek
  • 3 lime leaves
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp beef stock and salt
  • 1/2 tsp cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp ground pepper
  • 1 segment of galangal
  • 2 cm cinnamon
  • 1 tsp brown sugar
  • Lime
  • 500 ml coconut milk from 1/2 coconut
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 6 red onions
  • 2 hazelnuts
  • 1/2 tsp coriander
  • 3 large red chilies (remove seeds)
  • 2 cm turmeric
  • 3 cm ginger

Instruction

  1. The first step, wash the meat, cut it according to taste, give lime juice, then wash again.
  2. After that, puree the spices, then stir-fry the ground spices with lemongrass, lime leaves, bay leaves, cinnamon and galangal until cooked.
  3. Enter the meat, mix well, then add the coconut milk. Cook over low heat, stirring occasionally.
  4. Then add salt, broth, cumin, pepper and brown sugar, let stand until the spices absorb and the water shrinks.
  5. Taste correction, remove and serve.

Selendang Mayang ice

×

Ingredients

  • 500 ml coconut milk
  • 1 pandan leaf
  • 50 gr Hunkwe flour
  • 25 gr rice flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoon white sugar
  • Pandan paste
  • Red food coloring

Topping sauce ingredients:

  • 150 gr palm sugar
  • 50 gr white sugar
  • 100 ml water
  • 1 pandan leaf

Gravy ingredients:

  • 250 ml coconut milk (150 ml coconut milk + water)
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1 pandan leaf

Instruction

  1. Put coconut milk, white sugar, Hunkwe flour, rice flour, vanilla and salt into a container. Mix them well and bring it to a boil.
  2. Divide the mixture into five equal parts. Add pandan paste to one part. Add red food coloring to another part. Then leave the rest of the parts just as they are (white colored)
  3. Pour the green-colored mixture into the baking sheet (cover the base with a plastic first), then pour the white-colored mixture, and the red-colored mixture last.
  4. How to make the sauce: Put all the sauce ingredients into a container. Bring it to a boil and let the sugar melt.
  5. How to make the gravy: Mix all the gravy ingredients and bring it to a boil. Keep stirring them so that the coconut milk will not curdle.
  6. Serve the dessert with its sauce, coconut milk, and some ice cubes.

Kuwut ice

×

Ingredients

  • 1 liter coconut milk
  • 4 teaspoon ground coffee
  • 125 gram palm sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 pandan leaves

Instruction

  1. Place coconut milk, coffee, palm sugar, and pandan leaves in a pot. Bring to a boil on to medium heat, stir frequently to prevent the coconut milk from curdling. Once it boils and the palm sugar has all dissolved, turn off heat.
  2. Discard the pandan leaves, and strain into 4 serving glasses. Add some slices of toddy palm fruit to each glass. Topped with ice cubes if you prefer cold bajigur.

Indonesian Traditional Food

Indonesia is one of the largest countries in the world. This is what makes Indonesian cuisine very diverse, Indonesia has 34 provinces and each province has its own special food. Popular foods that are often heard are rendang, soto, satay, fried rice and chili sauce. some say that Indonesians cannot live without sambal. it's better to eat without side dishes than without chili, that's what he said. and if you visit each area you will definitely be spoiled by the culinary delights that are very diverse in taste. spicy, sweet, savory, salty. just choose which one you want.

Do you know?

SAMBAL

One of the uniqueness of Indonesian culinary is Sambal. Sambal is a mixture of chili with other ingredients such as salt, garlic, shallots, tomatoes, and others. chili sauce is usually a complement to food. it's not delicious yet if there's no chili sauce. Therefore, Indonesia has many variants of chili sauce, there are sambal terasi (shrimp paste), matah sambal , dabu dabu sambal, garlic sambal, and many others.
